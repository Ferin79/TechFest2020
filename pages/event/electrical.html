Line Follower: ELEC-LINEF

Min Team:2 Members

Max Team: 4 Members

Department: Electrical

Price: INR 50 per Head

No. of Rounds: 2


  • Event Description

    The objective of this contest is for a robot to follow a black line on a white background, without losing the line, and navigating several 90 degree turns. The robot to complete the course in the shortest period of time while accurately tracking the course line from start to finish wins.

  • Events Rules

    • A team may consist 2 to 4 members.
    • Only one robot is allowed per team.
    • Eligibility: All students with a valid ID card of their respective educational institute are eligible to participate.
    • Only wireless and remote less (no external control) robots are eligible to participate.
    • No power supply will be provided.
    • The line shall be a 15 to 25mm wide black coloured with white background.
    • Once a robot has crossed the starting line it must remain fully autonomous, or it will be disqualified.
    • If the robot does not move from one place for more than 2 minutes of time will be disqualified.
    • The robot should not damage the arena in any case, it can lead to disqualification.
    • Dimensional and weight limits for robots shall be strictly enforced. Robots must have passed inspection prior to competing.
    • Time is measured from the time the robot crosses the starting line until the time it crosses the finish line. A robot is deemed to have crossed the line when the forward most wheel or leg of the robot contacts or crosses over the line.
    • A maximum of 5 minutes in first qualifying round and 10 minutes in other remaining rounds is allowed for a robot to complete the course. A robot that cannot complete the course in the allotted time shall be disqualified. Only best 10 robots out of all are selected after completion of first qualifying round.
    • Time shall be judge with a stopwatch.
    • A robot cross the arena surface will be disqualified, A robot shall be deemed to have left the arena when any wheel or leg has moved completely off the arena surface.
    • Any robot that loses the line course must reacquire the line at the point where it was lost, or at any earlier (e.g. Already traversed) point.
    • Any robot that loses the line course and fails to reacquire it will be allowed a single reattempt. The robot must start the course again from the beginning, and if it loses the
    line course on its second attempt it will be disqualified. • The decisions of all officials regarding these rules and the conduct of the event shall be final.

  • Robot Specifications

    • Maximum Dimensions: 20cm X 15cm X 15cm (L x B x H).
    • Maximum weight : 3Kg
    • The weight mention above is including the battery weight.
    • Ready-made (company made) robots are not allowed.

Circuit Samiksha ELEC-CIRCUIT

Min Team:2 Members

Max Team: 2 Members

Department: Electrical

Price: INR 50 per Head

No. of Rounds: 2


  • Event Description

    The event “Circuit Samiksha” is based on the brain mapping of participants in the field of basic electrical and electronics engineering. The event involved theoretical as well as practical aspects based challenges. Different round for the event are as follows


    Round 1

    This round is based on the fundamentals knowledge of electrical and electronics engineering.


    Round 2

    Different challenging tasks will be given to participants related to basic electrical and electronics engineering. Within given time limitation tasks must be completed.


    Round 3

    This round is based on real implementation of assigned task within given time limit. Necessary hints and components will be provided at the time of competition.


  • Events Rules

    1. Each team consist of 2 participants.
    2. All the necessary components will be provided.
    3. Students should have the knowledge of basic electrical and electronics engineering.
    4. Event consists of three rounds.
    5. Disqualification rules:
    a. Any team not present at the allotted time will be disqualified by default.
    b. Use of mobile phone and Internet during the competition is strictly prohibited, if found that team will be directly disqualified at that moment.
    c. The decision of the organizers in declaring the results will be final. No queries in this regard in this regard will be entertained.
    d. In case of a conflict over a certain answer, the decision of co-ordinators will be final.
    e. Any member of a team found indulging in un-acceptable behaviour will attract disqualification for the entire team.

Maze Hunter ELEC-MAZE

Min Team:2 Members

Max Team: 4 Members

Department: Electrical

Price: INR 50 per Head

No. of Rounds: 2


  • Event Description

    • For all those 90’s kids who are fed up with seeing those memes that hit like if you remember this game and feels nostalgia here’s a chance to live those days back again when you used to play video games like Pacman, Rescue Rover, Dangerous Dave, Catacomb 3D and many others. Today, you just remember the scenario of the game but forgotten the name. But this time you are not going to play them in virtual world but you will play those games in real world. Like dodging the monsters, performing a task. So, let your inner child comes back again and explore those games in reality.
    • Participants will be able to learn team coordination, technical knowledge about robot construction,


    Round 1: Complete all task explained by event volunteers (Qualifying Round):

    The robot has to clear the hurdles and collect all points placed at different places on maze in minimum time. Qualification will purely base on time and minimum foul.


    Round 2 Face off (semi-final round )

    Two robots will compete to complete task in minimum time


    Round 3 FINAL ROUND :

    Two winners form semi-final round will play for final.


  • Evaluation Criteria

    • Judging will be done by event coordinators.
    • Penalties will be given if Hand touched while bursting , waste of time at a particular spot, if participants robot crosses the maze lines, if any type of external help done by participants to robot like pulling the robot backwards using wires connection.
    • The time will also be a major factor in case the tie occurs in the point system.
    • Decision made by the event head will be final and acceptable in any cases.
    • The decision of the coordinator will be final and the rules may vary for the successful complication of the event
    •Certificate of participation will be given to all participants

  • Events Rules

    • The Robot with remote will be provided by us.
    • Participants can use their own robots of given specifications(Dimension max 22x16x10, battery <=12 V, Weight < 1kg, strictly wireless)
    • The participants are not allowed to damage the track, or indulge in any argument with the coordinator as this will lead to disqualification of their robot.
    • Penalty will be given when robot moves out of arena.
    • The organizers reserve the right to change any or all of the rules as they deem fit. Changes in rules, if any, will be highlighted on the website.
    • Violation of any of the rules will result in immediate disqualification.
    • The maximum time limit for the Completion of Task inside the arena is 10 minutes.
    • Teams that are not ready when called for turn will be considered to have declared a walkover, and will receive no points.
    • In all cases, the judge’s decision will be final and binding.
    • The operator of the Robot is not allowed to step in the arena.
